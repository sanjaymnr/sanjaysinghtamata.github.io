Australia Awards Journey

Every great journey begins with a spark.

My story begins in Sudurpashchim, Nepal, where aspiring to study in a global university felt distant. Today, that journey connects a rural classroom to the Australia Awards and advanced study in computer science and cybersecurity.

As a child in Sudurpashchim, I cycled significant distances every day to attend school. The road was long and demanding, but it taught me resilience, discipline, and the value of education. Growing up, I saw how limited access to technology and opportunities constrained many capable students.

Over time, I became the first in my family to study engineering, completing a Bachelor of Computer Engineering at the Institute of Engineering, Pulchowk Campus. This opened the door to serve as a Computer Engineer in the Government of Nepal, working across central and provincial agencies on information systems, automation, and e-governance.

Learning about the Australia Awards Scholarship gave structure to a goal I had carried since childhood: to connect global knowledge with local public service. The scholarship closely aligned with my intention to strengthen digital governance, data-driven decision-making, and secure public systems.

In February 2025, I began my Master of Computer Science (Cybersecurity specialisation) at the University of Sydney. This program allows me to deepen my technical understanding of cybersecurity, software engineering, and data, with a clear focus on returning to Nepal and translating these skills into practical reforms.

Commitment I am committed to returning to public service in Nepal, applying what I learn in Australia to build stronger digital systems, support fair investigations, and help citizens access transparent and reliable services.
